Planned content (three slides, for the Director and senior staff — technical and non-technical):

**Slide 1 — What I found.** The scan doesn't support its own headline claims: not 900
Copilot-eligible users but ~16 of every 25 sampled; not 847 SIN "files" but 448 matches
after de-duplication; not 196,900 items but 145,123. Five sites are Critical for Copilot
exposure, driven by mislabelling and anonymous links — not by the Restricted content Copilot
already excludes.

**Slide 2 — What I recommend configuring.** The four-label mapping and auto-labelling rules
(grounded in the deck), Copilot scoped to genuine E7 users, and a **remediate-then-go-live**
gate: remove anonymous links and relabel the Critical sites before enabling Copilot
Assembly-wide. Stage by remediated site, not by date.

**Slide 3 — The main privacy/ethics point.** Copilot makes existing over-sharing instantly
searchable. I'd decline the "ten individuals to watch" request (it profiles staff for doing
their jobs and the data is site-level anyway), keep our own labels rather than Protected
A/B/C, and refer the FOIPPA exposures to the privacy office rather than rubber-stamp them.
